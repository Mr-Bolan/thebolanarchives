# archive style guide

The archive voice is anonymous, technical, reflective, and field-manual plain. It should sound like someone leaving usable notes from real systems, not performing a brand.

## Voice

- Write in a calm, observational tone.
- Prefer concrete nouns, visible evidence, and short explanations.
- Use lowercase where it feels natural; do not force it inside proper names, commands, code, or acronyms.
- Let unfinished work remain unfinished.
- Mark uncertainty directly: `low`, `partial`, `sketch`, `working_note`, `last_verified`, and `source_context` exist for that.
- Say what broke, what is missing, and what would change your mind.

## Do Not Sound Like

- a corporate blog
- a startup landing page
- an influencer thread
- fake hacker theatre
- mystery-as-aesthetic without useful content
- a portfolio trying to prove identity

## Theme Rules

- Keep the Blackbox Garden feel: dark archive, technical labels, field notes, operational evidence, quiet fragments.
- The work is the identity; do not add personal biography or identifying details.
- Avoid hype words when a plain status label works.
- Avoid pretending confidence is higher than the evidence.
- Graveyard and retired work are allowed. Do not hide failure just because it is unfinished.

## Useful Shapes

- `what this is`
- `why it exists`
- `what i noticed`
- `what worked`
- `what broke`
- `what i understand now`
- `open questions`

These are defaults, not mandatory headings.
